What you need to know

ELECTRIC PICNIC TAKES PLACE FROM FRIDAY 30TH AUGUST - SUNDAY 1ST SEPTEMBER 2019.

Map

Age Policy

You must be aged 18 years or over to attend Electric Picnic Music and Arts Festival, with the exception of children aged 12 and under. As in previous years, 13-17 year olds are not permitted to attend the event even when accompanied by an adult. It is not acceptable to turn up to the festival with children aged between 13-17 and anyone who is of this age will not gain admission to the festival.
Children aged 12 years and under must be accompanied by a ticket holding adult throughout the weekend, limited to a maximum of two children per adult. Children aged 12 years and under do not require a ticket but they must be registered before arriving at the festival. If you are bringing your child and feel they look older than 12 years old please bring ID to back this up (colour photocopies will suffice).
Those wishing to stay in the Family Campsite must be accompanied by children aged 12 years and under.

Tickets


Wristbands

  • The wristband tents are located just inside each campsite gate so you need to swap your ticket for a wristband before you enter the campsite and pitch your tent.
  • Re-admittance to the site is only possible once you have got your wristband. Entrance to the arena is only possible with a valid wristband. There is no readmission on day tickets.
  • Wristbands removed from the wrist or tampered with will be rendered invalid and will not be replaced.
  • To avoid any disappointment we advise all ticket holders to leave plenty of time to get their wristband and allow time to walk to the arena and the stages to avoid missing any of your chosen acts.
  • We advise all day ticket purchasers to arrive early to avoid missing any of their chosen acts and to leave ample time to get from the day arena entrance to the stages.

Electric Picnic 2019 has a range of accommodation offerings. The amazing Pink Moon Village will be back for yet another year to please the thousands of Picnic fans with some great structures to go alongside the wonderful facilities which they offer.

The Arena

Its more than music festival

  • Art: Explore a world of art and creation at Electric Picnic. Throughout the arena you will find sculptures and art work hidden in every nook and cranny. Over the years we have seen creations such as the Giant Gramaphone, Washing Machine Maze, Goat Mountain, The WyrdWood Vilage, Fiddling Fox and Wicker People.
  • Comedy Tent: Prepare yourself for a belly full of laughs as we provide you with some of the best established and up-and-coming comedians from around Ireland.
  • Greencrafts:​The Greencrafts village is a brilliant place to spend a while changing gear and making something beautiful to take home. Specializing in traditional (or some say forgotten) crafts – the organisers Nic and Marie have brought together some of Ireland’s most exciting craft workshops, so you can choose from over forty things to make from silver rings, to wooden plates, felted scarves to iron pokers, slate pendants to willow baskets...and loads more.
  • Global Green: On the edge of the Picnic you will discover the Global Green, the conscious heartbeat of Electric Picnic. It connects people to one another and creates a vision of possibilities, with engaging conversations, uplifting performances and food for the soul.
  • Mindfield:​ The Mindfield spoken word arena boasts a stimulating spark-filled programme designed to entertain, inform and inflame. Set among the ancient trees on the lawns of Stradbally Hall you'll find an oasis of literary readings, political debate, performance poetry, science experiments, inspiring talks, news quizzes, public conversations and much more. The Mindfield Arena invites us to fill our brains with new thoughts, ideas and experiences that we hope will engage and help inspire us to all be change makers.
  • Theatre of Food: Located in the Mindfield arena of Electric Picnic, the Theatre of Food features a line-up of chefs, writers, thinkers, artisans, brewers, bakers, and food heroes. It's a celebration of everything we love about food and drink - and music. Over the weekend you can expect food demonstrations, mixology, "Theatre of Food Tastes" treating the audience to delicious tastings of exciting foods almost every hour, talks, manifestos, food quizzes and much more.
  • Fossett's Circus​: You will be transported into a world where aerialists defy death, jugglers defy gravity, acrobats defy description and the clowns simply defy everyone.
Family Info


Family Campsite

The Family Campsite is our dedicated campsite for families with children aged 12 or under (limited to 2 children per adult). Please note that ID checks will be in place at the festival, if you feel your child looks older than 12 years old please bring ID to back this up (colour photocopies will suffice). There are showers, toilets and water points available in this campsite.
Only people with Family Camping tickets are permitted to stay in the Family Campsite. No single or groups of adults will be permitted entry to the campsite unless accompanied by a child under aged 12 or under. It is also not acceptable to turn up to the family campsite with children aged between 13-17. Anyone who is of this age will not gain free admission to the festival or otherwise. Anyone who arrives without a child under the age of 12 will need to camp in the general campsites. Please note that ID checks will be in place at the festival, if you feel your child looks older than 12 please bring ID to back this up (colour photocopies will suffice).
People with Family Camping tickets have access to our dedicated Family Car Park which is located a short distance from the campsite making it easier to carry your equipment/luggage from your car.

Disabled Access


Facilities

The Disabled Access Campsite is available to customers who require accessible facilities. By locating these facilities in one campsite we can ensure that they are serviced, cleaned and maintained regularly. You can apply to camp here by completing an Access Requirements Form. The campsite is located as close as possible to the arena and is staff by the Access Team 24 hours a day throughout the event. Security personnel and stewards are also based in the campsite.
Disabled Access Campsite Facilities:

  • Wheelchair accessible unisex showers and toilets
  • Standard toilets and showers
  • Drinking water points and accessible sink
  • Electrical points for charging wheelchairs or mobility scooters
  • Fridge for secure medication storage


Party Size As well as you and your PA, we will allow two friends to camp with you. You must register the number of your group during the application process so that they are issued with the appropriate wristbands to access the Disabled Access Campsite.

Greencraft

Totally off-grid, the area celebrates and teaches the ancient techniques and crafts of our wilder forefathers. Greencrafts is bursting with energy and environmental skill-share which inspires new thinking about ethics and environmental sustainability. Curated by Nic and Marie Piper and brought to you by master craftsmen and women, this is the place to come and learn a traditional skill, get hands-on with natural and sustainable materials and make something beautiful to take home with you. The village doesn’t use any electricity – everything is hand or solar powered, and all materials are sourced ethically, being sustainable and genuinely environmentally stable.
